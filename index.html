Who We Are

Background of ISKCON

Founded in 1966 by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, ISKCON — the International Society for Krishna Consciousness — is a worldwide spiritual movement rooted in the ancient Vedic tradition of India.

Srila Prabhupada brought the teachings of Bhakti Yoga and the Hare Krishna Maha Mantra to the world at the age of 70, transforming lives across cultures and continents with his extraordinary vision and compassion.

ISKCON's mission is to promote spiritual knowledge, foster a community of devotees, and serve all living beings as part of the divine family of God — connecting each soul to the eternal truth of Krishna Consciousness.

What We Do

Our Activities & Mission

📿

Hare Krishna Maha Mantra

The chanting of the Hare Krishna Maha Mantra is the primary spiritual practice — a transcendental sound vibration that purifies the mind, calms the heart, and connects the soul with the Supreme.

🎶

Harinaam Sankirtan

Public congregational chanting of the holy names of God. Devotees take to the streets with musical instruments, spreading joy, peace, and divine vibration to all who hear.

📖

Vedic Scriptures

The Bhagavad Gita As It Is and Srimad Bhagavatam are the foundational texts. ISKCON has distributed millions of copies, bringing ancient wisdom to modern readers worldwide.

🌍

Food For Life

One of the world's largest vegetarian food relief programs. Established in 1974, it serves millions of free, sanctified meals each year to those in need — regardless of background.

🧘

Bhakti Yoga & Well-Being

ISKCON's spiritual practices — mantra meditation, yoga, vegetarian diet, and community — provide powerful tools for mental peace, emotional resilience, and inner fulfillment.

🙏

Devotional Service

Every action performed as an offering to God — cooking, cleaning, singing, teaching — becomes devotional service (seva). ISKCON encourages all to discover their unique way to serve.

Sacred Food

ISKCON Prasadam

Prasadam — food offered to God with love — is central to ISKCON. It nourishes body and soul, and is freely distributed as an act of compassion and spiritual generosity.

"No one should go hungry within a ten-mile radius of an ISKCON temple."
— Srila Prabhupada
Global Compassion

Food For Life
ISKCON's Humanitarian Program

Established in 1974, Food for Life is one of the world's largest vegetarian food relief organizations. Inspired by Srila Prabhupada's vision that no one should go hungry, it operates across numerous countries.

  • 🍱 Nutritious vegetarian meals for school children
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Support for low-income families
  • 🌊 Disaster relief operations worldwide
  • 🏠 Meals for homeless and vulnerable populations
  • 💚 Promoting healthy, plant-based nutrition

Millions of meals served annually — with volunteers from all walks of life united by compassion.

Mind & Soul

ISKCON & Mental Well-Being

Bhakti Yoga — the path of devotional service — offers powerful, time-tested tools for psychological health, emotional resilience, and inner peace in a chaotic modern world.

🧘

Meditation & Japa

Daily chanting of the Hare Krishna mantra on japa beads calms the mind, reduces anxiety, and cultivates inner stillness. Comparable to mindfulness but enriched with devotion.

🌿

Stress Reduction

Spiritual teachings redirect attention from anxiety-causing materialism toward purpose and gratitude. Research shows that spiritual practice significantly lowers cortisol and stress markers.

🤝

Community & Belonging

The ISKCON community (sangha) provides a warm, supportive network. Social connection is one of the strongest predictors of mental health and emotional well-being.

🌟

Purpose & Meaning

Devotional service gives life profound direction. Having a spiritual goal — serving God and humanity — cultivates resilience, optimism, and a deep sense of fulfillment.

Radha Govinda Gallery

Sacred Texts

Timeless Wisdom

Bhagavad Gita As It Is

The eternal dialogue between Lord Krishna and Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra — a guide for living a life of wisdom, duty, and devotion.

Srimad Bhagavatam

The "Ripened Fruit of the Vedic Tree" — 18,000 verses celebrating the glory of God and the path of pure devotion. Srila Prabhupada's monumental translation spans 30 volumes.
